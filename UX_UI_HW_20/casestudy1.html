Play It Forward



A redesign project for Play it Forward, a nonprofit that helps young students of music acquire instruments such as pianos, and guitars that they would otherwise not have access to.



Problem



Play It Forward (Snowman Foundation) was designed to give children access to music through instruments and instruction due to lack of funds in schools. We have observed that the platform needed to continue these efforts isn’t shedding enough light on the importance of donating instruments, & money, which in return lessens the impact Play It Forward can make on children’s lives. How might we improve the platform so that more people would be willing to donate to children wanting to learn music?





Hypothesis



To better understand our goals for the website redesign we needed to understand the goals of “PIF”. Their mission is centered around giving access to music education to any interested child. This included instrument donations, as well as music lessons. After evaluating the site we hypothesized , that by improving the Play it Forwards donation form, overall usability, and style. As well as simplifying the user flow, we could make it easier for the charity to receive and give donations.





Moodboard









User Interviews







In interviews, The people we spoke to acknowledge the inherent importance of music, as well as it’s educational benefits, and found a general willingness to donate an instrument. From these interviews two groups of people emerged those who would use the site to receive music benefits and those who would love to contribute and donate.





“I would donate an instrument to a kid in need. I would most likely buy a new one to give away.”
“Music moves the spirit and the body.”
“Music helps teach kids basics (i.e ABCs) and makes it fun!”

Wireframes





Previously, the homepage and about page seemed at odds with each other, so we combined the two. This early wireframe version clearly lays out the navigation across the top, provides a mission statement, and the stats offer a quick understanding of what the nonprofit does at a glance. The donate form includes an interactive way to visualize what your monetary donation will become.

Prototype





When it came to designing we went with a playful design that captured the youth that this program is for, while also keeping a modern feel.







Donations Forms





The old donation forms seemed daunting and a little lengthy. Breaking them down into steps and showing the progress simplified the process.



Conclusion



After iterating and testing our prototype there was a mutual consensus that the site was a good way to gather the information needed for this non-profit. Not only is it playful and welcoming but it provides information in a palatable way for consumers.
